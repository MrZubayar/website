01
Introduction
Course overview · exam format · certificate validity
1 lesson ▼

Welcomes the learner, explains the course structure, the EASA open-category framework, and the competencies required to pass the A1/A3 online theory exam.

1.1
Welcome & Course Overview
Video Lecture
~4 min ▼

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the purpose and scope of the EASA A1/A3 certificate
  • Navigate the course structure and know what to expect in each module
  • Identify the final online theory exam requirements (minimum 75% pass mark)

Key Topics

  • What is EASA and why does it regulate drones?
  • The three UAS operation categories: Open, Specific, Certified
  • Where the A1/A3 certificate fits within the Open Category
  • Exam format: 40 MCQ, 30 min, 75% required

Instructor Notes

Keep this segment motivational. Remind students that the exam is online and self-paced. Encourage them to attempt the mock exams before the final.

02
Drone Regulations
EU legislative framework · subcategories · privacy · insurance · security
9 lessons ▼

Covers the full EU legislative framework governing drone operations in the Open Category, including subcategory rules, operator responsibilities, privacy, liability, incident reporting, and security.

2.1
Creation of Regulations
Video Lecture
~5 min▼

Learning Objectives

  • Explain why a harmonised EU drone regulatory framework was introduced
  • Identify the primary regulations and their entry-into-force dates
  • Distinguish between EU-level regulation and national NAA rules

Key Topics

  • History of drone incidents that prompted EASA regulation
  • EU Regulation 2019/947 (operations) and 2019/945 (class marks)
  • Transition periods and the 2024 applicable requirements update
  • Role of National Competent Authorities
  • Key definitions: UAS, UA, remote pilot, operator

EASA Reference

EU Reg 2019/947 Art. 1–3 · EU Reg 2019/945 Art. 1–4 · EASA Easy Access Rules (July 2024)

Instructor Notes

Use a timeline graphic to illustrate the regulatory milestones from 2019 to 2024. Clarify that member states may add stricter local rules on top of EASA minimums.

2.2
Open Category & Subcategories
Video Lecture + Interactive Quiz
~11 min▼

Learning Objectives

  • Define the Open Category and state its max weight and altitude limits
  • Differentiate between subcategories A1, A2, and A3
  • Match UAS class marks (C0–C4) to each subcategory

Key Topics

  • MTOM ≤ 25 kg · altitude ≤ 120 m AGL
  • VLOS requirement (~500 m horizontal)
  • A1: C0/C1 — may overfly uninvolved persons
  • A2: C2 — 30 m (or 5 m low-speed) from people
  • A3: C2/C3/C4 — ≥150 m from populated areas
  • Legacy & privately built aircraft rules
  • Operator registration requirements

EASA Reference

EU Reg 2019/947 – UAS.OPEN.010, .020, .030, .040 · EU Reg 2019/945 Annexes

Instructor Notes

Most examined topic. Use a decision-tree diagram for subcategory selection. Include scenario-based quiz questions. Learners often confuse subcategory with class mark — address directly.

2.3
Aircraft Without Class Marking
Video Lecture
~2 min▼

Key Topics

  • Legacy (grandfathered) aircraft: purchased before 1 Jan 2024
  • Privately built UAS rules by weight
  • No CE class mark required — but operational conditions still apply
  • Compliance declaration via national registration portal

Instructor Notes

Many learners own a legacy drone. Clarify these aircraft are fully legal but the pilot must know which subcategory conditions apply by weight.

2.4
Open A1 & A3 Requirements in Detail
Reference Summary
~1 min▼

Key Topics

  • A1: may overfly uninvolved persons — never over assemblies
  • A3: 150 m safe distance from built-up areas
  • Minimum age: 16 years (12 with supervision in some states)
  • Online A1/A3 training + theory exam mandatory
  • Operator registration + registration number displayed on drone

Instructor Notes

Summarise as a quick-reference checklist. Clarify 'operator registration' vs 'drone registration' — a common source of confusion.

2.5
Responsibilities of Remote Pilot & Operator
Video Lecture
~7 min▼

Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish operator responsibilities from remote pilot responsibilities
  • List pre-flight and in-flight obligations from the regulation
  • Identify what constitutes a lawful refusal to fly

Key Topics

  • Operator: registration, insurance, SOPs, geo-awareness
  • Remote pilot: fitness, manual familiarity, pre-flight checks, VLOS
  • Duty of care to uninvolved persons and property
  • No flying under alcohol or psychoactive substances
  • Right and duty to abandon an unsafe flight

EASA Reference

EU Reg 2019/947 – UAS.OPEN.060, UAS.OPEN.070 · AMC1 UAS.OPEN.060

Instructor Notes

Use real-world vignette scenarios. Ask: 'You arrive on site and a temporary restriction has just been issued — what do you do?' Reinforce the pilot's authority to refuse unsafe flights.

2.6
Privacy & Data Protection
Video Lecture
~4 min▼

Key Topics

  • GDPR applicability to aerial imaging (faces, plates, properties)
  • Lawful basis: consent, legitimate interest, public task
  • Privacy by design: minimise, encrypt, delete
  • Data subject rights: access, erasure
  • National variations and DPA guidance

Instructor Notes

Provide a practical checklist: inform → minimise → secure → delete. Use a relatable scenario (wedding photographer drone). GDPR fines are real.

2.7
Liability & Insurance
Reference Summary
~1 min▼

Key Topics

  • EU Reg 785/2004: third-party liability mandatory for operators
  • Cover: bodily injury, property damage
  • Minimum cover varies by MTOM
  • Household/contents insurance rarely covers drone liability

Instructor Notes

Stress that most recreational pilots underestimate this requirement. A specialist drone insurance policy is typically needed.

2.8
Incident & Accident Reporting
Video Lecture
~2 min▼

Key Topics

  • EU Reg 376/2014: mandatory vs voluntary occurrence reports
  • Accidents: report to NAA within 72 hours
  • Voluntary near-miss reporting and just-culture principles
  • ECCAIRS database and safety lessons sharing

Instructor Notes

Reassure students: voluntary reporting is protected by just-culture. Show the national reporting portal URL for the learner's country.

2.9
Security
Video Lecture
~2 min▼

Key Topics

  • Drone misuse risks: surveillance, contraband, airspace collision
  • Remote ID broadcast requirement
  • Geo-awareness and geo-fencing compliance
  • Sensitive areas: airports, military, critical infrastructure
  • Reporting suspicious use of drones

Instructor Notes

Link to the liability lesson: a stolen drone used maliciously can still expose the registered operator to legal risk.

03
Air Safety
Dangerous goods · meteorology · environment · UAS tech · human factors
9 lessons ▼

Develops the knowledge required to operate safely in shared airspace — meteorological assessment, UAS technical understanding, airspace classification, and human-factor risk management.

3.1
Dangerous Goods
Video Lecture
~3 min▼

Key Topics

  • ICAO Dangerous Goods: LiPo as Class 9 hazmat
  • Safe storage voltage (3.8 V/cell), thermal runaway risks
  • Transport rules: hand-luggage LiPo limits on aircraft
  • Payload considerations: sprayers, drop systems
  • Disposal: approved recycling centres only

Instructor Notes

Use a LiPo fire video to reinforce safe handling. Provide a battery storage checklist as a downloadable resource.

3.2
Meteorology for Drone Pilots
Video Lecture + Quiz
~10 min▼

Learning Objectives

  • Read a basic weather forecast and assess its flight implications
  • Identify meteorological hazards that ground a flight
  • Interpret wind speed using the Beaufort scale

Key Topics

  • Wind: speed, gusts, wind shear, turbulence
  • Beaufort scale (consumer drones: Beaufort 4–5 max)
  • Temperature effects on battery (below 0 °C / above 40 °C)
  • Fog, mist, cloud ceilings and VLOS reduction
  • Density altitude: high temp + high altitude = reduced lift
  • Micro-weather: urban canyons, coastal thermals
  • Sources: METAR, TAF, UAV Forecast, Windy

Instructor Notes

Heavily examined area. Use animated weather maps. Include a METAR interpretation exercise for go/no-go decision. Mention seasonal hazards: frost, summer convection.

3.3
Environment & Nature
Video Lecture
~7 min▼

Key Topics

  • Wildlife disturbance: nesting birds, marine mammals, livestock
  • Protected areas: Natura 2000, national parks, SSSI
  • Noise pollution and community impact
  • Leave No Trace principles for drone pilots
  • Positive environmental uses: surveys, disaster response

Instructor Notes

Balance restrictions with positive environmental use cases. Encourage pre-flight research on local nature designations.

3.4
UAS Technical Knowledge – Part 1: Aircraft Systems
Video Lecture
~10 min▼

Key Topics

  • Multirotor configurations: quad/hex/octo and redundancy
  • ESCs, brushless motors, propellers
  • Flight controller: IMU, barometer, GPS, magnetometer
  • GNSS: GPS, GLONASS, BeiDou — multipath errors
  • RTH and failsafe behaviours

Instructor Notes

Use an exploded-view diagram of a consumer multirotor. Link sensor knowledge to pre-flight checks (compass calibration, GPS lock count).

3.5
UAS Technical Knowledge – Part 2: Power & Communication
Video Lecture
~11 min▼

Key Topics

  • LiPo: cell voltage, capacity (mAh), C-rating
  • SoC vs SoH · cycle counting · temperature management
  • Safe battery level for RTH: ~30 % SoC
  • RC link: 2.4 GHz / 5.8 GHz, interference sources
  • Signal loss failsafe: RTH, hover, or land
  • Electromagnetic interference near power infrastructure

Instructor Notes

Demonstrate a battery voltage checker. Provide a battery log template. Flying with a degraded battery is a common cause of flyaways.

3.6
UAS Technical Knowledge – Part 3: Performance & Limitations
Video Lecture + Quiz
~12 min▼

Key Topics

  • Payload and wind effects on endurance
  • Cold reduces battery capacity by up to 30 %
  • Obstacle avoidance: ultrasonic, stereo vision, ToF — blind spots
  • Maintenance: propeller inspection, motor bearings, firmware
  • End-of-life indicators: cracks, vibration, swelling

Instructor Notes

Worked example: 30 min rated drone, 2 °C, 20 km/h headwind, gimbal payload — what is the realistic endurance? Walk through the calculation step-by-step.

3.7
Flying Over People
Video Lecture
~4 min▼

Key Topics

  • Definition: uninvolved person vs participant
  • Assembly of people: density prevents escape
  • A1 C0/C1: may overfly — never directly above head
  • A2: 30 m lateral (5 m low-speed mode)
  • A3: 150 m from all populated areas
  • Kinetic energy: KE = ½mv² — why mass and speed both matter

Instructor Notes

Use kinetic energy figures: a 250 g drone at 10 m/s carries ~12.5 J — comparable to a cricket ball. Subcategory choice must reflect the environment before take-off.

3.8
Airspace Classification & Risks
Video Lecture
~3 min▼

Key Topics

  • ICAO airspace classes A–G
  • CTR, TMA, ATZ — when permission is required
  • Restricted, Prohibited, Danger areas — hard no-fly
  • NOTAMs and Temporary Restricted Areas
  • Geo-zones: EU Reg 2021/664 (U-space)
  • Apps: Drone Assist, Airmap, DJI FlySafe
  • Drone pilots give way to all manned aircraft

Instructor Notes

Practical exercise: have students look up the airspace around their home address using a national geo-awareness tool before the next lesson.

3.9
Limitations of Human Factors
Video Lecture
~6 min▼

Learning Objectives

  • Identify human-factor threats most linked to UAS incidents
  • Apply IMSAFE and PAVE checklists to personal readiness

Key Topics

  • SHELL model: Software, Hardware, Environment, Liveware
  • Situational awareness loss as leading incident precursor
  • Get-home-itis, mission pressure, fatigue, stress
  • IMSAFE: Illness · Medication · Stress · Alcohol · Fatigue · Emotion
  • PAVE: Pilot · Aircraft · enVironment · External pressures
  • CRM adapted for two-person drone operations

Instructor Notes

Use a short anonymised accident report to illustrate how human factors chain together. IMSAFE is frequently examined — students must recall all six items.

04
Flight Procedures
Pre-flight · in-flight · post-flight
3 lessons ▼

Standardised procedures for the complete flight cycle — planning and pre-flight checks, in-flight management of normal and abnormal situations, and post-flight actions.

4.1
Pre-Flight Planning & Checks
Video Lecture + Demonstration
~6 min▼

Key Topics

  • Airspace check: geo-zone, NOTAM, ATC permission if required
  • Weather assessment: wind, gusts, visibility, temperature
  • Drone inspection: propellers, motors, structural integrity
  • Battery check: SoC ≥ 80 %, no swelling, voltage per cell
  • GPS lock ≥ 8 satellites · compass calibration on new site
  • Emergency plan: identify landing zones, brief second crew
  • Documentation: operator ID displayed, certificate accessible

Instructor Notes

Provide a downloadable printable pre-flight checklist PDF. Run a timed demo of a complete pre-flight in under 5 minutes to show it's practical, not burdensome.

4.2
In-Flight Procedures
Video Lecture
~9 min▼

Key Topics

  • VLOS discipline: scanning technique, sun angle, orientation cues
  • Altitude management: below 120 m AGL
  • Airspace deconfliction: immediate descent if manned aircraft detected
  • Signal loss: RTH activation — don't panic
  • Low battery: land without delay at nearest safe spot
  • GPS degraded / ATTI mode: manual attitude control
  • Compass error: land immediately — risk of fly-away

Instructor Notes

Simulate signal-loss and low-battery scenarios. Reinforce: RTH altitude must be set above the tallest obstacle in the area before take-off.

4.3
Post-Flight Procedures
Video Lecture
~3 min▼

Key Topics

  • Landing: confirm motors stopped before approach
  • Battery: cool before charging · log cycle count
  • Storage charge: ~3.8 V/cell if not flying within 48 hrs
  • Post-flight drone inspection for damage
  • Flight log: date, location, duration, serial number
  • Charging safety: never unattended — use LiPo-safe bag

Instructor Notes

Share a real case where a missed post-flight defect caused an incident on the subsequent flight. Post-flight care directly extends drone lifespan.

05
Mock Examinations
Exam practice · question bank · gap analysis
3 lessons ▼

Provides exam practice under realistic conditions to prepare learners for the official EASA A1/A3 online theory test. Two full 40-question mock exams with topic-mapped feedback.

5.1
Exam Briefing Video
Video Briefing
~1 min▼

Key Topics

  • Format: 40 multiple-choice questions · 30 min · 75 % pass
  • Question domain weighting
  • Unlimited re-sits via national NAA portal
  • Certificate: 5 years validity, tied to operator registration
  • Strategy: read each question twice, eliminate wrong answers

Instructor Notes

Keep reassuring. The exam tests applied knowledge, not pure recall. Remind students it's open to all — even if they've never touched a drone.

5.2
Mock Examination 1
Online Quiz (40 questions)
30 min▼

Topic Breakdown

  • 10 Qs — Drone Regulations (subcategories, class marks)
  • 8 Qs — Airspace & NOTAMs
  • 8 Qs — Meteorology & Environment
  • 7 Qs — UAS Technical Knowledge
  • 4 Qs — Human Factors
  • 3 Qs — Flight Procedures, Privacy, Insurance, Security

Instructor Notes

Display score immediately with per-question feedback. Flag questions in error for the Revision Bank. Question weighting mirrors the official EASA AMC.

5.3
Mock Examination 2
Online Quiz (40 questions)
30 min▼

Key Points

  • Full syllabus — randomised from a 200+ question bank
  • Scenario-based questions: real operational dilemmas
  • Increased difficulty: edge cases and less common regulatory articles
  • Target: ≥ 75 % as reliable predictor of final exam readiness

Instructor Notes

If a learner scores below 75 % on both mocks, generate a personalised revision plan flagging weak domains. Gate access to the Final Examination until ≥ 75 % on at least one mock.

06
Final Examination
Official end-of-course assessment
1 lesson ▼

The official end-of-course assessment. Passing this examination demonstrates that the learner has met the EASA A1/A3 theoretical knowledge requirements under EU Regulation 2019/947.

6.1
Final Examination
Formal Online Examination
30 min▼

Objectives

  • Demonstrate competency across all EASA A1/A3 knowledge domains
  • Achieve minimum 75 % score for certificate issuance

Format

  • 40 questions across all course modules
  • Randomised question and answer order
  • No revision materials permitted
  • Immediate results on completion
  • Digital certificate issued upon passing
  • Results submitted electronically to national NAA

Instructor Notes

Verify learner identity before the exam commences. Store results securely in compliance with GDPR. Unsuccessful candidates receive a gap analysis and may re-attempt after 24 hours.
